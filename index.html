Learning and Development

  • Tailored training packages as per the organisational need related to supporting people with a Disability/mental health condition. Holistic approach to support.
  • Tailored recruitment packages specifically for the Disability and Mental Health sector.
  • Self defence programs not related to the work place. Women self defence programs (developing a 10 week program). Focus is on environmental awareness, self protection and survival.Practical not theory based.
  • PART Training (Predict, Assess and Respond to Challenging Behaviours). Addresses Legal frame work, systems approach, self awareness/reflection and self defence for workers confronted with people who display challenging and/or aggressive behaviour. Not just for Disability and Mental health workers.Theory and practical. (1 day theory course, 2 day practical and theory course, 3 day theory, practical and restraint course).

Support systems review

  • Establishing a baseline for supports - implementing a systemic approach to supports then tailoring the persons individual needs around the resources available such as staffing, budget and environments.
  • The organisation will be provided with a comprehensive impartial view of their service and recommendations to improve the service and practice standards.
  • Additional support can be arranged to implement the system onsite with staff with a quality assurance element completed 3 months after the closure of the service.
  • Developed staff support focusing on staff retention by creating a supervision, mentoring and leadership approach that adheres to Work Health Safety and good wellbeing.
  • linking the service to external providers to work with the individual Service User related to Positive Behaviour Support and Psychological services which include therapies and counselling for children and adults.

Person Centred Approaches

  • Active support principles to encourage people to get the best out of life and the service that provides them with support.
  • Training staff - Person centred support planning principles and development of ongoing processes to establish and achieve goals.
  • Mediating/ chairing Person Centred Planning meetings with all stakeholders.
  • Consulting with organisations at a operational level to develop consistent Person Centred Planning principles.
  • Specific staff recruitment support and selection.

Restrictive Practices Review

  • Review of current restrictions in place and discussion of alternative support systems that encourage independence and reduce the restrictions placed on people by use of choice and developing the persons understanding of choice and risk.
  • Review of risk where restriction may be required.
  • Review of risk to staff and adherence to WHS legislation to promote a safe and harmonious workplace.
  • Tailored training programs for staff to develop their knowledge of Restrictive Practices in NSW and Australia with the view to reduce the restrictions to the point of elimination.

Accredited and endorsed PART trainer by MTU Concepts - include hyperlink and attachment from MTU of PART training outcomes and resources. PART is the systemic approach to creating a safe environment when supporting people who display challenging and/or aggressive behaviours. (This has been authorised by MTU already).
